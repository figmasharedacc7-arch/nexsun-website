# TwoSuns.ai Keyword Master List

One place for both keyword sources, kept separate on purpose:

- **Part 1, Brand and category terms** confirmed with the product team. These are how we *talk* (positioning, category creation, thought leadership). Most have little organic search demand, which is fine: their job is brand, not traffic.
- **Part 2, Target keywords** from the SEO audit (`docs/SEO-audit-2026-06-11.md`). These are what we try to *rank* for. Vertical-qualified long-tail where a new domain can realistically win.

The single most important idea: **brand vocabulary and SEO targets are not the same list.** Use Part 1 for headlines and Part 2 for page titles, H1s, and new pages. Where they overlap (see "system of decision vs system of record"), that is the sweet spot. **Part 4** is the deployment plan that ties the two together: each brand term gets paired with a real search keyword and a home on the site.

Companion docs: `docs/SEO-keyword-map.md` (per-page primary/secondary map), `docs/SEO-audit-2026-06-11.md` (full audit).

**Demand legend:** HIGH, MEDIUM, LOW, NEAR-ZERO. Reasoned from SERP signals during the 2026-06-11 research, not exact volumes. Validate in Ahrefs or Semrush before allocating budget. **Intent:** I = informational, C = commercial.

## Part 3, Do not target for SEO (handle with care)

- **Intelligent Ops / "intelligent operations":** maps to AIOps in the SERP. Brand label only; rank pages on "operational intelligence"/"industrial AI".
- **System of Decision Records, System of Outcomes, one-word "Glassbox":** near-zero demand. Brand IP, not traffic. Do not measure them by rankings.
- **Saturated head terms:** agentic AI, AI governance, decision intelligence (bare), predictive maintenance (bare), supply chain AI / visibility (bare), manufacturing AI (bare). Owned by hyperscalers, analysts, and entrenched incumbents. Win the vertical-qualified long-tail underneath them (Part 2).

## Part 4, Deployment plan for the spine (product team, with SEO pairing)

Each spine term gets a job, a paired search keyword, and a home on the site. This is the plan, not yet deployed. On-page copy changes wait on positioning sign-off and an explicit go-ahead.

| Term | What it says | Pair with (search keyword) | Where on site |
|---|---|---|---|
| System of Decision Records | The category. Beyond the system of record, you capture every decision and its rationale (the SoDR concept, now Core). | "decision intelligence", "system of record alternative" | Homepage category line, Platform / Core, How It Works |
| System of Outcomes | Tied to results, not activity. | "outcome-based AI", "operational outcomes" | Value prop, homepage, ROI / pricing |
| Intelligent Ops | The domain. AI-run operations for industry. | "operational intelligence", "industrial AI", "intelligent operations" | Homepage, Industries, Solutions |
| Human in the Loop | AI augments people, never replaces them. | "human-in-the-loop AI" | Why TwoSuns (HITL Governance card), Trust, Technology |
| Glassbox AI | Every decision transparent and auditable. | "explainable AI", "auditable AI" | Trust, Technology, How It Works |

**SEO refinement (which pair member to actually rank the page on):**
- **System of Decision Records:** rank on "decision intelligence" (real demand) and the long-tail "system of record alternative". The brand term carries the headline; the keyword carries the title/H1/meta.
- **System of Outcomes:** both paired keywords are LOW demand. Treat this page as brand-led and rank it on a value/vertical term (for example "operational ROI for heavy industry"), not "outcome-based AI".
- **Intelligent Ops:** rank on "operational intelligence" and "industrial AI". Keep "intelligent operations" out of titles/metas (AIOps collision); fine in visible copy.
- **Human in the Loop:** rank on the qualified "human-in-the-loop AI for industrial operations", not the contested bare head term.
- **Glassbox AI:** rank on "explainable AI"/"auditable AI", industrial-scoped. Glassbox is the brand label for the same idea.

## How to use this list

| Goal | Use | Where |
|---|---|---|
| Brand, positioning, category creation | Part 1 (+ 1d phrases) | Headlines, hero copy, About, investor materials, thought leadership |
| Rank and capture traffic | Part 2 | Page titles, H1s, meta descriptions, new landing pages, glossary |
| Tie brand to ranking, page by page | Part 4 | The spine pages (Core, Trust, Technology, How It Works, Industries) |
| Both at once (the sweet spot) | "System of decision vs system of record", "AI orchestration layer for complex industries" | A glossary hub plus the homepage category line: brand language that also has a real, winnable SERP |

The fastest format to rank in this space is a tight "what is X" explainer (40 to 60 word definition + FAQ block). Definitional SERPs here are owned by vendor glossaries, not Wikipedia, so a vendor can legitimately win the snippet.
