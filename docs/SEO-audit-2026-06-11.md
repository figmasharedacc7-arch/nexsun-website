# TwoSuns.ai SEO Audit, 2026-06-11

Full-site audit of the static TwoSuns marketing site (31 real pages + 3 dev pages). On-page and technical findings come from parsing the actual source files (`/Users/mohammaddidarulalam/Documents/Claude/nexsun/*.html`), so they reflect what ships, not a guess. Keyword and competitor sections come from live web research (14 searches). No SEO tool (Ahrefs/Semrush) is connected, so demand tiers are reasoned from SERP signals, not exact volumes. Site is not yet in Search Console, so "current ranking" is "not indexed" across the board.

Companion docs: `docs/SEO-keyword-map.md` (per-page keyword map), `docs/SESSION-HANDOFF.md` (project state).

---

## Executive summary

The technical foundation is genuinely good: clean sitemap, self-referencing canonicals to twosuns.ai, 100% image alt-text coverage (359 images), mobile viewport everywhere, baseline schema on nearly every page, no em dashes and no "real time" anywhere. That is a stronger base than most early-stage sites.

The problem is not the plumbing, it is the signal. Three issues hold the site back, and none of them is the positioning sweep everyone is waiting on:

1. **The site still reads as an energy-trading product, not an industrial-ops product.** "Trading" appears in 12 of 31 pages; ETRM, CTRM, EFET, ISDA, and NAESB sit on the homepage and core pages. Search engines will classify the site against the keyword targets it is trying to win (cement, mining, manufacturing). This cleanup is safe to do now: those terms are wrong under both "complex industries" and "complex organizations," so it is not blocked by the positioning decision.
2. **The money pages are buried or orphaned.** The three `solutions-*` pages and `resources.html` have zero inbound internal links. The cement and mining landing pages have one inbound link each, and `industries.html` is not in the global navigation. The whole industrial story is hard to reach for both crawlers and visitors.
3. **The invented brand spine has near-zero search demand.** System of Decision Records, System of Outcomes, Intelligent Ops, and one-word "Glassbox" capture no organic traffic. They are brand vocabulary, not SEO targets. Real, capturable demand lives in vertical-qualified long-tail (cement and mining especially), where competitors are thin, low-authority programmatic pages.

Overall assessment: **strong foundation, needs focused content and internal-linking work.** Two live meta-description bugs ("complex enterprises enterprises", "raw complex enterprises data") should be fixed today regardless of anything else.

3. **What is decision intelligence in manufacturing.** Why: emerging term, no entrenched glossary owner, snippet up for grabs. Format: "what is" explainer with FAQ block. Priority high, effort quick.
4. **Predictive maintenance for mining equipment (haul trucks, conveyors, crushers).** Why: only Uptake/Dingo compete seriously; equipment long-tail is open. Format: mining landing + equipment sub-pages. Priority high, effort moderate.
5. **Predictive maintenance for cement plants.** Why: clear commercial intent, thin competition. Format: landing section + mini case study with hard numbers. Priority high, effort moderate.
6. **Glass box AI in industrial / operational AI.** Why: generic glass-box explainers exist, none operations-specific. Natural home to introduce the brand term. Format: glossary entry. Priority medium, effort quick.
7. **Human-in-the-loop AI for industrial operations.** Why: head term owned, process-control qualifier open. Format: blog/explainer. Priority medium, effort moderate.
8. **Predictive maintenance ROI calculator.** Why: real searches, almost no interactive tools in this niche. Link magnet and lead gen. Format: interactive calculator. Priority medium, effort substantial.
9. **Comparison pages: "Palantir Foundry alternatives for heavy industry", "C3 AI alternatives".** Why: comparison SERPs are all third-party today, a vendor can occupy them. Keep factual. Format: comparison pages. Priority medium, effort moderate.
10. **Vertical AI for cement / mining.** Why: rides a hot 2026 term while escaping its saturated head via the vertical qualifier. Format: thought-leadership pillar. Priority medium, effort moderate.

The single highest-ROI format across this whole space is the "what is X" explainer with a tight 40-60 word definition plus an FAQ block. Definitional SERPs here are dominated by vendor glossaries (IFS, Aras, IBM, project44), not Wikipedia, so a vendor can legitimately own the snippet.

## Competitor comparison

TwoSuns has no single head-on competitor; it sits where three SERP clusters overlap (industrial-AI platforms, predictive-maintenance/IIoT, decision-intelligence/supply-chain). The realistic competitive set for the keyword targets:

| Dimension | TwoSuns | C3 AI | Cement cluster (iFactory, oxmaint, CemAI, Ripik) | Augury |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Vertical landing pages | 2 (cement, mining), thin and buried | Strong `/industries/` set | High volume via programmatic templates | Narrow (machine health) |
| Content depth | Low-moderate (645-670 words on industry pages) | High | Low per page, high page count | High in PdM |
| Glossary / "what is" coverage | None | Some | Some programmatic | Strong |
| Comparison / "vs" pages | None | None (third parties write them) | None | None |
| Domain authority | New, near-zero | High | Individually low | Strong in niche |
| Technical / CWV | Good base, image weight to fix | Strong | Mixed | Strong |
| SERP features owned | None | Some brand terms | Some long-tail | Featured snippets in PdM |

Where TwoSuns can win: the cement vertical (thin, low-quality competition, clear quality arbitrage), the still-open definitional terms ("system of decision vs system of record", "decision intelligence in manufacturing"), and the comparison/"alternatives" SERPs that no vendor currently occupies. Where it should not fight: head terms owned by C3/SymphonyAI/Augury/project44 and the hyperscaler-saturated trend terms.
