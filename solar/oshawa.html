Solar Energy Landscape in Oshawa

Oshawa, Ontario is positioning itself as a solid solar candidate with moderate irradiance. With a population of 175,000 and annual solar irradiance of 1230 kWh/m², the city offers moderate conditions for residential and commercial solar installations.

Climate and Solar Performance

Oshawa's humid continental climate brings warm summers and cold winters. Solar panels actually perform better in cooler temperatures, meaning Oshawa's cold, clear winter days can generate surprisingly strong output. Summer months deliver peak generation with long daylight hours extending past 9 PM in June and July.

Electricity Costs and Savings Potential

With electricity priced at 13.0¢/kWh, Oshawa homeowners face meaningful energy costs that solar can significantly offset.

The economics are straightforward: a 10 kW system in Oshawa runs approximately $24,500 before incentives. With estimated annual savings of $1,820, homeowners can expect a competitive payback period for Canadian solar.

Incentives and Programs in Ontario

Oshawa homeowners have access to the Canada Greener Homes Grant. Ontario's net metering program and the Canada Greener Homes Grant support solar adoption in Oshawa; the city's detached housing stock offers ample rooftop solar potential.

Federal programs, including the Canada Greener Homes Grant, may provide additional rebates for qualifying installations. Nexsun.ai's platform tracks all available programs to ensure homeowners capture maximum value.
