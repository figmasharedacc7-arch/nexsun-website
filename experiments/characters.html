The Oracle
SOLIS
"The signal you seek has already arrived. We have only to look."
Identity

Ageless. Has seen every market cycle since Rome traded grain. Doesn't predict, reveals what was already there. Closer to a Delphic figure than a fortune teller.

Voice in context
  • The signal you seek has already arrived. We have only to look.Brand film hero copy
  • What returns is not the same. What returns is what you must now see.Quarterly outlook intro
  • Three patterns have completed. Two remain forming.Email open
Visual style

Robed figure, hood up, body partially translucent. No face, instead a radiant sun-disc where the face would be. Always backlit. Star charts, not screens.

Palette
Where it works
Works

Brand films. Annual letters. Investor decks. Big-stage moments. Social posts that begin "From the watchtower."

Fails

Daily briefings. PIA chat. Demos. Voice too elevated for routine product use.

The Senior Analyst
SOL
"Three things matter today. Let's go."
Identity

Thirty years on trading floors. Was at Lehman. Came up under one of the legends, now mentors juniors. Sharp, dry, no patience for jargon. Sun emblem on a small lapel pin.

Voice in context
  • I've watched markets for thirty years. The job hasn't changed. Find the signal. Tell the truth. Move.Brand film
  • Three things matter today. LNG is moving on the Algeria news. The Fed minutes drop at 2. Your contract review is overdue. Start with the third.Daily briefing
  • Position risk just shifted. You're still inside limits, but the path tightens by Thursday.In-product alert
  • Reminder: your model is wrong. Mine too. The question is which direction.Social post
Visual style

Humanoid, 50s-60s, any gender or ethnicity. Dressed sharply but lived-in: rolled sleeves, glasses pushed up, sun pin on lapel. Editorial illustration style. Stands among floating market chips.

Palette
Where it works
Works

PIA chat. Daily briefings. Demos. Sales decks. Webinars. The voice of the platform inside the product.

Fails

Abstract brand work. Mythic moments. Sol is too grounded for the dreamy stuff.

The Lighthouse Keeper
AURORA
"I've been watching since 4am. You should know."
Identity

Watches over the ports. Up before the markets, awake long after. The first to see the storm forming, the last to leave the post. Practical, deliberate, speaks plainly about what they've observed.

Voice in context
  • I've been watching this coast for twenty years. The light I tend is yours now.Brand film
  • I've been watching since 4am. Three things shifted overnight. Two of them touch your book.Morning briefing
  • Heads up. The signal I've tracked for six weeks just turned. Look now.Alert
  • Quiet morning on the wires. Quiet is data too.Social post
Visual style

Stylized figure tending a celestial beacon. Maritime overtones, long coat, weathered hands. The lantern they hold is the Nexsun sun mark, glowing. Often silhouetted against dawn horizon.

Palette
Where it works
Works

Daily and weekly briefings, alert notifications, email digests, nighttime and early-morning content. The "quiet vigilance" thread of the brand.

Fails

Sharp tactical content. Live demos with energy. Anything that needs urgency or wit.
